# Diet Pattern Reference

Diet pattern constraints shape what foods and meal templates are acceptable.

## Balanced diet

A balanced eating pattern usually combines lean protein, vegetables, fruit, whole grains, legumes, and healthy fats.

## Vegetarian

Vegetarian planning excludes meat and fish. Protein can come from tofu, tempeh, beans, lentils, dairy, eggs, Greek yogurt, nuts, and seeds when those foods are allowed.

## Vegan

Vegan planning excludes all animal-derived foods. Protein sources often include tofu, tempeh, soy milk, beans, lentils, edamame, seitan, nuts, seeds, and fortified foods.

## Low lactose or dairy free

Users may still tolerate yogurt, lactose-free milk, or hard cheese, but this varies. The assistant should clarify whether the user has lactose intolerance, dairy allergy, or a general preference.

## Gluten aware

If a user requests gluten-free meals, prioritize rice, potatoes, quinoa, oats labeled gluten-free, legumes, vegetables, fruit, eggs, and unprocessed proteins.
