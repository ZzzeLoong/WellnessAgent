# Food Allergy Safety Basics

Food allergy planning must treat known allergens as hard constraints, not preferences.

## Core principles

- Exclude confirmed allergens from meal plans.
- For severe allergies, consider cross-contact risk from shared equipment, sauces, and packaged foods.
- When recommending restaurant or takeaway options, remind the user to verify ingredients and preparation methods.
- If a user mentions a history of anaphylaxis, the assistant should avoid making casual substitution assumptions.

## Common examples

- Peanut allergy: avoid peanuts, peanut butter, peanut oil unless clearly refined and medically confirmed as safe for that user.
- Shellfish allergy: avoid shrimp, crab, lobster, shellfish sauces, and mixed seafood dishes.
- Dairy allergy: avoid milk, cheese, butter, cream, whey, and casein.
- Egg allergy: avoid obvious egg dishes and hidden ingredients in baked goods, sauces, and dressings.

## Planning rule

Before giving a final meal suggestion, check whether any ingredient conflicts with the user's allergy list or likely cross-contact risks.
