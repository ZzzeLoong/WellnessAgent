# Fat Loss Nutrition Basics

Fat loss meal planning should focus on sustainability, adequate protein, and satiety.

## Common guidance

- Use a moderate calorie deficit rather than an aggressive crash diet.
- Prioritize protein at each meal to support satiety and muscle retention.
- Emphasize high-volume foods such as vegetables, broth-based soups, fruit, and legumes.
- Reduce liquid calories, frequent desserts, and highly processed snacks when they displace more filling foods.

## Meal template

- Protein source: chicken, fish, tofu, eggs, Greek yogurt, beans, or lean meat
- Fiber source: vegetables, salad, fruit, beans, oats, or whole grains
- Carbohydrate source: rice, potatoes, oats, quinoa, or whole-grain bread
- Fat source: avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil, or nut butter in controlled portions

## Coaching note

If a user reports strong hunger, low energy, or difficulty adhering to the plan, adjust meal structure, food volume, and snack strategy before recommending stricter restriction.
